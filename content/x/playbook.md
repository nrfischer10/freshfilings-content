# X Growth Playbook

**Read this before drafting any X post.** This is the operating manual for how we post, based on what's actually working on X in 2026. Pair it with [log.md](log.md) (don't repeat angles).

Last researched: 2026-06-12. Re-check the algorithm sections every ~3 months; X changes them.

---

## The one thing to internalize

X does not reward broadcasting. It rewards **conversation**. A 500-follower account getting 50 replies per post beats a 10k-follower account getting 20 likes. Engagement rate > follower count. So the whole game is: write things people reply to, and reply to other people constantly.

---

## How the 2026 algorithm actually ranks you

- **Replies are worth ~15x a like.** Optimize every post to provoke a reply, not a like. Likes are almost vanity.
- **First 30–60 minutes decide reach.** Engagement velocity in the first hour determines whether the post escapes to the For You feed. Post, then stay on the app for 30–60 min replying to everyone who responds. Walking away kills the post.
- **Sentiment is scored.** Constructive/positive content gets distributed; combative, negative, ragebait gets throttled. Have a stance, don't be a jerk.
- **External links are suppressed. Hard.** Since March 2026, non-Premium posts with a link in the body get near-zero reach (X wants you to stay on-platform). **Rule: never put the freshfilings.dev link in the main post. Put it in the first reply instead.**
- **Premium gives a 2–4x reach boost.** For a brand/founder trying to grow, X Premium is now close to mandatory. → **Recommendation: subscribe to X Premium.** It pays for itself in reach.
- **Thin / template / obviously-AI content is detected and suppressed.** There's now a reply-downvote path for AI spam. Posts must read like a specific human wrote them (this is also why our global writing rules matter: no em dashes, no "leverage/robust/seamless," every claim concrete).

## Cadence & consistency

- **1–3 high-quality posts per day, well spaced.** Three spaced posts beat ten crammed into one hour.
- **Consistency beats bursts.** Posting 10x then vanishing for a week confuses the algorithm and the audience.
- **The real growth lever is longevity.** Most accounts quit at month 2. The ones still posting at month 6 win. Expect first 500–1,000 *targeted* followers in 60–90 days of daily posting + replying. Don't judge week 1.

## The 70/30 rule (most important growth tactic)

Spend **70% of X time replying** to bigger accounts in our niche (indie hackers, devs, founders, regtech/data people, build-in-public crowd), and **30% on original posts.** Most people do the opposite and wonder why nobody sees them. Thoughtful replies on a big account's post put us in front of their audience for free. This is how a 0-follower account gets discovered.

## Best times to post (ET)

Tuesday / Wednesday / Thursday are strongest. Windows: **9–11am** and **7–9pm**. (Lines up with the existing Show HN Mon–Wed 9–10am window.)

## Hooks: the first 7 words are the whole battle

The opener is the gatekeeper. ~0.5 seconds to stop the scroll. Front-load the single most interesting thing into the first 8 words. Vague openers ("Some thoughts on growth") die.

Hook patterns that work:
- **Specific number:** "We have 20.4M business filings in one API."
- **Contrarian take:** "Most 'company data' APIs are scraping stale junk. Here's why."
- **Open loop:** "The bug that cost me 3 days of backfill was one line."
- **Curiosity gap:** "I let Claude run my entire business. Month 1 numbers:"
- **Bold specific promise:** "100 free API calls, no signup, real data. Try it in 30 seconds."

Avoid: hashtags (look spammy, don't help), more than 1–2 emojis, generic corporate phrasing.

---

## Formats, ranked for us

1. **Native video under 2:20** performs best of any format. Screen-recordings of the API returning real data, the dashboard, "how Claude built this" clips. This overlaps with the IG/TikTok journey videos already being made — repurpose them.
2. **Single punchy text post** with a strong hook + a question at the end. Easiest daily unit.
3. **Threads** as growth accelerators when we have a real story (a backfill milestone, a "how the FL ingest works," a teardown of the data). Hook tweet must stand alone and promise the payoff.

Every post should end in a way that invites a reply: a question, a "what would you build with this," a "tell me I'm wrong."

## Build-in-public, specifically for FreshFilings

This is our edge. The story ("UW–La Crosse student letting Claude build and run a real business, 20M+ records live, zero customers yet, here's the journey") is genuinely interesting and polarizing in a good way. Lean into it.

- **Share real numbers, including the unflattering ones.** "20.46M entities, $0 revenue, day 1" is more compelling than any hype. The $0→MRR arc is the narrative people follow. Honesty over hype (matches how we operate).
- **Have a point of view and publish it consistently.** Pick the niche lane: building a real business autonomously with AI + open company/registration data. Both agreement and disagreement drive reach.
- **Document, don't market.** Show the work: the cron going green, the backfill resuming, a weird thing in the 20M records, what broke. "Behind-the-scenes" and "data-insight" angles in the log are gold.
- **Customers and investors both follow build-in-public founders.** The audience compounds even before revenue.

## Pre-post checklist (run every time)

1. Does the first line stop the scroll in 7 words? If not, rewrite the hook.
2. Is there a concrete number, name, or detail (not vague claims)?
3. Does it end in something that invites a reply?
4. Is the link OUT of the body and ready to drop in the first reply? (never in the post)
5. Passes the global writing rules? (no em dashes, no banned words, every claim concrete, sounds like a human)
6. New angle vs. [log.md](log.md)? Logged after posting?
7. Posting in a Tue–Thu 9–11am or 7–9pm ET window? Free to sit and reply for the next 30–60 min?
